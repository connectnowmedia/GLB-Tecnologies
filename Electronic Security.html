CCTV, or closed-circuit television, is a video surveillance system used for security purposes. It is widely used in public areas, commercial buildings, and private residences to monitor and record activities. CCTV systems use cameras to capture footage, which is then transmitted to a recording device or monitoring station.

There are two types of cameras used in CCTV systems: analog and IP cameras. Analog cameras are the traditional type of CCTV camera and use a wired connection to transmit footage to a recording device. They are affordable, easy to install and operate, and are ideal for small-scale surveillance systems.

IP cameras, on the other hand, are digital cameras that use the Internet to transmit footage to a recording device or monitoring station. They offer higher image quality and resolution than analog cameras, and can also be accessed remotely through the Internet. IP cameras are ideal for large-scale surveillance systems or for locations where high-quality footage is necessary.

CCTV systems can also include other components such as digital video recorders (DVRs) or network video recorders (NVRs) to store and manage footage, as well as additional features such as motion detection, night vision, and pan-tilt-zoom capabilities. With the increasing demand for security, CCTV has become an essential tool for ensuring safety and protecting property in a variety of settings.
